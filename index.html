Eight Ways of Paying Tribute to Samuel Barclay Beckett

  • One - Samuel Barclay Beckett was born in Dublin, Ireland on 13 April 1906.
  • Two - He was an Irish novelist, playwright, short story writer, theatre director, poet, and literary translator.
  • Three - He is considered one of the last modernist writers, and one of the key figures in what Martin Esslin called the "Theatre of the Absurd."
  • Four - His best-known work is his 1953 magisterial play, Waiting for Godot
  • Five - He was awarded the 1969 Nobel Prize in Literature "for his writing, which—in new forms for the novel and drama—in the destitution of modern man acquires its elevation."
  • Six - On 10 December 2009, the new bridge across the River Liffey in Dublin, Ireland was opened and named the Samuel Beckett Bridge in his honour.
  • Seven - An Post, the Irish postal service, issued a commemorative stamp of Beckett in 1994. The Central Bank of Ireland launched two Samuel Beckett Centenary commemorative coins on 26 April 2006: €10 Silver Coin and €20 Gold Coin.
  • Eight - Died on 22 December 1989.
